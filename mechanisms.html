Are there mechanisms that explain why these different types of behaviours have evolved?


When investigating potential reasons that explain evolution of different types of courtship behaviours, there are three potential answers: the demographics of the species or population at question, the mating systems of the species, and the parental care that the species partakes in. While these three concepts can be possible explanations to the diversity of courtship behaviours, there are still many other explanations that can be considered that will not be investigated on this website.

Demographics

     What this section is referring to, is the structure of the population of the species in a particular area. This is important in regards to courtship styles because it influences the amount of sexual selection occurring in the population, as well as if the male or female will participate in the courtship rituals or be selective. Operational sex ratio (OSR) is the ratio comparing males to females in a population (Kvarnemo, 1996). When the OSR is high it indicates there are more males in a population than females. As a result, males would be more likely to sexually select for females with desirable traits or behaviours (Kvarnemo, 1996). When the OSR is low, there are more females than males. A low OSR leads to female sexual selection, and competition between males for mates (Kvarnemo, 1996). 

    The dynamic of the population in regards to is demographic influences sexual systems of a species and the types of mating rituals that will be conducted. This is an example of how different courtship behaviours have evolved because it demonstrates variation may stem from differences in population demographics of a species.

Mating Systems

    As mentioned, multiple times previously, the general difference between species and their mating systems has a substernal influence on the diversity in courtship behaviours. Some examples different mating systems, are monogamy, polygamy, and polymorphic species. A polyamorous species, is a species with individuals who have more than one mate. This would change from a species that is monogamous, which means they only have one mating partner per season, or lifetime. Polymorphic mating systems have males with different morphologies, meaning they all look different, that all have different reproductive behaviours depending on their size or appearance. The variety of mating strategies in the animal kingdom contributes to the amount of diversity in courtship behaviours because different animals have different goals of reproductive success.

Parental Care

    Similar to mating strategies, parental care can also be incorporated to the cause of diversity in courtship styles. For example, different species have paternal, maternal, or joint care of offspring. The care of offspring is in turn related back to the demographic of the population. To explain, a population with a high OSR will be likely to have maternal care because the males will want to leave and copulate with as many females as possible. Populations with a low OSR will be more likely for paternal care of offspring, or joint care of offspring because there are fewer males present and they want to protect their genetic livelihood. An example of how parental care influence courtship behaviours can be found when looking at a species with paternal care of offspring. Males will likely be involved in competition between other males to mate with females, or have a mating system that is regulated by female sexual selection. This will lead to a more aggressive style of courtship behaviours, as the male is trying to show off.



    Demographics, mating systems and parental care, are likely not the only reasons to explain why different types of courtship behaviours have evolved. These examples do however work to explain some mechanisms involved that increase the amount of diversity related to sexual behaviours in animals. The ratio of males to females influences the amount of sexual selection or competition within the population, which causes differences in courtship rituals to evolve. The mating systems of different animals is influences by the OSR of the population, and will affect the parental care of the offspring. The differences in mating systems and parental care will allow for diversity in reproductive behaviours to arise.
