About Project

    The animal kingdom contains a tremendous amount of diversity. Physiological characteristics, biological processes, and the behaviour of individuals and species are all examples of the differences that exist in animals. All animals have behaviours such as, selfishness, cooperation, aggression, and altruism. There is also diversity in reproductive and courtship behaviours. Animal courtship behaviours occur when individuals are interested in reproducing. These are specific rituals or behaviours that signal to the opposite sex that they are interested in mating with them. In other words, courtship rituals in animals is a way of impressing the opposite sex in hopes that they will mate with them.

    Diversity of courtship can exist in the types of display, such as a aesthetic showing of bright colours, attractive movements or vocalizations, or a display of aggression or strength. The diversity in courtship behaviours can also stem from the individual(s) who display them! In some species the male participates in the courtship displays, however in others, it is the female who tries to woo the male. There are also species where both males and females put on the mating display.

    The diversity of courtship behaviours in the animal kingdom, can be broadly compared to the mating behaviours of humans! With some exceptions of course! In humans, the goal of courtship and copulation is not always to reproduce. Humans have unique cognitive abilities, that allow us to have complex relationships with the people around us, making our courtship styles more complex than multiple species combined.

In this interactive website, we will explore some interesting questions surrounding the diversity of courtship rituals and reproductive behaviours. Diving into the question: Why is courtship so diverse? and Are there reasons that explain why different types of behaviours have evolved? We will also look at different interesting examples of courtship styles in the animal kingdom, and compare them to those found in humans as an added bonus.
