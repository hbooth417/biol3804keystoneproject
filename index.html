Diversity of Courtship Behaviour throughout the Animal Kingdom



Animals are a diverse and complex group of living organisms. The diversity exists in all aspects of life, including the way individuals’ mate and reproduce. Courtship rituals and  reproductive behaviours differ from species to species, however all of such behaviours  universally serve the same purpose. This paper describes multiple explanations as to why  courtship behaviours are so divers, as well as what mechanisms may in action to drive the  evolution of these behaviours.



ABOUT PROJECT GO TO QUIZ

It is possible that courtship behaviours have become so diverse because of evolution and  the creation of new species with similar but changed courtship behaviours. Adaption may also be  playing a roll in diversity in reproductive behaviours, as changing environments change the value  of certain behaviours compared to another. Game theory explains the above point by explaining  that organism will likely preform behaviours that are most beneficial to them depending on their  environment. Mechanisms such as operational sex ratio, mating systems, and parental care of a species’ offspring may all be examples of underlying reasons why courtship behaviours have become so diverse. While there is no certain cause for the diversity of courtship behaviours,  there are several mechanisms acting on organism through time and space that help us understand  the evolution of the diversity of courtship behaviours.
